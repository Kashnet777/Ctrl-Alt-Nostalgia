Before I start, I wanna tell you how things were before.

Before lockdown I was in a group where I technically had friends like Ved, Mayank, even Ansh Singh, but it never really felt like I belonged there. I always felt like a weirdo. I used to get defensive whenever someone pointed out my mistakes, and somehow I genuinely believed I knew everything (I didn't know shit... but you get my point, right? 😭).

Lockdown was a bad experience for most people, but for me it was like going down a rabbit hole. It was during that time that I realised what was happening in my own family, what some of my relatives had done in the past, and a lot of things I had never noticed before. At the same time I was obsessed with joining the Army, especially the Special Forces, so I started pushing myself physically and mentally to develop the qualities I thought I needed. (Yes... the 7–10 days without sleeping. Don't kill me 😭.)

With everything happening at once, I slowly started losing trust in people I once trusted blindly. I also convinced myself that emotions were a weakness. Somewhere along the way, I lost my natural smile. I hated getting my pictures clicked, got scolded for always looking serious (haan... pita bhi hoon 😭), and by the time I came back to school I had become so used to faking a smile or laugh that it just felt normal.

Now after... or more like when I met you... it started with simple classmates wali conversations. Nothing special. Mostly school work. But after 10th and 11th, when our group formed and we started talking more, things slowly changed.

That was the time I actually wanted to trust you and talk to you about anything, but I was scared of being betrayed again, so I kept my trust limited to Aadya only. But as time passed, we talked more, I got to know you, and I realised that even the cool and fun kids have problems of their own.

I started trusting you. And you know... while talking to you, I never felt like an outcast. You accepted me the way I was (not like you could've gotten a different version anyway 😭), and that meant so much to me that you probably don't even realise it.

I actually started letting myself feel things again. I was finally ready to talk about things I didn't want to talk to anyone else about. And I genuinely think I'm a better person today than I was before. There's still a lot left to accomplish, but the credit for that mostly goes to you.

I have said this before and I'm saying it again...

I OWE YOU FOR THAT!!
