Chapter IX · The Letter

Letter

no tape. no doodles. just words.

Written sometime late. You know what time.

I've been trying to write this for a while.

I kept starting and deleting and starting again because nothing felt like enough. And then I realized that's sort of the point — that some things are bigger than the words you have for them, and you just have to write them anyway.

You became one of my favorite people without either of us noticing. One day it was small talk. Then it was 3 AM calls. Then it was the first person I think of when something is funny, or bad, or strange, or just worth saying out loud.

I'm glad for the unfinished plans and the debates that went nowhere and the inside jokes that require twelve sentences of context to explain to anyone else. I'm glad for the calls where nothing important was said, and also for the ones where everything important was said and we both pretended not to notice.

I don't have a neat ending for this. Which feels right, honestly.

You know the rest. You were there for all of it.

— still here
